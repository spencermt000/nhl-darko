# The ELC Advantage — Why Draft Picks Matter

Entry-Level Contracts (ELCs) are the most important tool in NHL roster construction. A first-round pick on an ELC can provide significant annual surplus value — the equivalent of getting a star player well below market rate.

## The Numbers

Using our multi-metric surplus model (v2), ELC contracts average **+1.91% of cap surplus per season**. That's compared to near-zero surplus for UFA signings. At a ~$95M cap, that's roughly **+$1.8M in annual surplus** per ELC player.

## Why This Matters for Team Building

The best teams in the NHL aren't just the ones with the most talent — they're the ones with the most **surplus**. A team with three ELC players producing at replacement level or above essentially gets 3 roster spots at minimum cost, freeing up $15-20M in cap space to spend on established talent.

This is why the draft is so important. A first-round pick that becomes an NHL regular generates roughly **$5-6M in annual surplus** during their ELC. That's the equivalent of signing a $5M/year UFA for free.

## The Draft Pick Value Chain

1. **Draft pick** → Prospect development (2-3 years)
2. **ELC years** → Maximum surplus value (3 years, ~$1M AAV)
3. **Bridge deal** → Still surplus, but less (~$3-5M AAV, RFA leverage)
4. **Long-term extension** → Fair value, surplus shrinks
5. **UFA** → Market rate, zero expected surplus

## Implications

- **Tank for picks:** The math supports it. Top-5 picks have dramatically higher expected surplus than picks 15-30.
- **Don't rush to sign extensions:** Every year on an ELC is essentially free money. Let the clock run.
- **Trade for young RFAs:** Players in their first 1-2 RFA years still have significant surplus value. They're the best trade targets.
