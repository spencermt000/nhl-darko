# Surplus Value — What Players Are Really Worth

Every NHL contract is a bet: the team pays a fixed cap hit hoping the player's production justifies the cost. Surplus value measures whether that bet paid off.

## The Formula

**Surplus = Market Value - Cap Hit**

- **Market Value** = (WAR × $/WAR) + minimum salary
- **$/WAR** is calibrated each season from the UFA free-agent market: total UFA cap spend above minimum / total UFA WAR produced

This yields roughly $16-20M per WAR, or about 19-21% of the salary cap per win.

## What We Found

**By contract type:**
- **ELCs** average +$908K surplus/season — cheap contracts on young talent are the best value in hockey
- **RFAs** average +$744K — the restricted market keeps prices below market value
- **UFAs** average ~$0 — the free market prices correctly on average (by definition)

**Biggest career surplus:** Connor McDavid at +$193M total surplus across 8 seasons. His $12.5M cap hit was a historic bargain for the best player in hockey.

**Worst contracts:** Erik Karlsson and Drew Doughty at $11M+ with negative WAR — each costing their teams ~$10M/yr in negative surplus.

## Why It Matters

Surplus value is the foundation for evaluating trades, draft picks, and roster construction. A team's total surplus across its roster is essentially its "cap efficiency" — how much production it's getting per dollar spent. Cup contenders tend to have large surplus from ELC players and team-friendly deals on stars.
