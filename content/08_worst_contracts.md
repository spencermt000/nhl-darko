# The Worst Contracts in the NHL (2025-26)

## What Went Wrong?

**The "Paid for Potential" Problem:** Pettersson ($11.6M) and Power ($8.35M) signed massive extensions based on projected growth that hasn't materialized this season. Pettersson's WAR dropped from historic levels to 0.10 — a catastrophic decline.

**The "Legacy Deal" Problem:** Doughty ($11M), Karlsson ($11.5M), and Seguin ($9.85M) are on deals signed 6+ years ago when they were elite players. Age caught up, but the cap hit didn't adjust.

**The "Injury Tax" Problem:** Matthews ($13.25M) has elite per-game production but has only played 60 games. His $13.25M cap hit still counts for 82 games worth of cap space.

## The Silver Lining

Not all "overpaid" contracts are bad management decisions:
- **Matthews** is still producing at a top-10 rate when healthy. The contract is underwater on a per-season basis but would look fine with 75+ GP.
- **Dahlin** at 24 has time to grow into his deal. This could age well.
- **Pettersson** is likely a regression outlier — his underlying metrics suggest a bounce-back.

## Context Matters

Our model treats each season independently. A player who's -$5M this year but projects to be +$2M for the next 4 years is still a net positive contract. The NPV model captures this — check the Contract NPV analysis for the full-lifecycle view.
