# Team Construction — What Wins in the NHL?

Using Win Shares, surplus value, and the Gini coefficient, we can study how winning teams are actually constructed.

## The Gini Coefficient for Win Shares

The Gini coefficient measures inequality of distribution — in our case, how evenly a team's wins are distributed among its players. A Gini of 0 means every player contributes equally. A Gini of 1 means one player accounts for all wins.

**What we observe:**
- Most NHL teams have a WS Gini between 0.35-0.55
- Teams built around one dominant star (Edmonton with McDavid) tend to have higher Gini
- Deep, balanced teams (Florida, Dallas) tend to have lower Gini
- There is **no strong correlation** between Gini and winning — both balanced and star-driven teams can succeed

## The Surplus Budget

Every team has a salary cap of ~$95.5M (2025-26). But the real competitive resource isn't cap space — it's **surplus value**. Teams with more surplus can effectively deploy more talent per dollar.

Where does surplus come from?
1. **ELC players** (+$1.9% of cap per season on average)
2. **Team-friendly extensions** signed during RFA years
3. **Veterans on discount deals** (e.g., players chasing a Cup)

Where does surplus leak?
1. **UFA overpays** (the "UFA premium" is real)
2. **Long-term deals that age badly** (8-year contracts signed at 30+)
3. **Injured players on full cap hits**

## Roster Archetypes

Based on our data, winning teams tend to follow one of two models:

### The "Core + Depth" Model
- 2-3 star players on fair-to-cheap deals
- 6-8 middle-class players on bridge or early UFA deals
- Fill the bottom of the roster with ELC players and league-minimum vets
- **Example:** Florida Panthers — Tkachuk + Reinhart + Barkov core, supported by cheap depth

### The "Superstar + Bargains" Model
- 1-2 generational talents consuming 15%+ of the cap
- Surround them with cheap, high-upside players
- Accept thin depth as the tradeoff
- **Example:** Edmonton Oilers — McDavid + Draisaitl eat 27% of cap, rest of roster filled with bargains

## The Math of Contention

A rough rule of thumb from our data:
- **82+ point pace** (playoff contender) requires ~35-40 total Win Shares from skaters
- A typical team gets 8-12 WS from their top player, 5-8 from their second, and 2-4 from each of the next 5-6
- Teams that miss the playoffs typically have their top player under 8 WS or lack depth (fewer than 4 players above 3 WS)
